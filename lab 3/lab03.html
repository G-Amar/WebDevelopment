Comparing HTML/CSS and Bootstrap for Responsive Design

Regarding challenges faced, both versions had different challenges. For HTML/CSS, writing out all the classes for the columns became tedious, but allowed for much more customization. For Bootstrap, ther was less boilerplate code but customizing was a little more work. Overall, both methods have their pros and cons, and I can see how someone would prefer one method over the other.

Personally though, I prefer the pure HTML/CSS method of responsive design. Despite the Bootstrap method overall being a little easier and faster, I enjoy having more control over the format and presentation of the website that I am creating. This was a fun assignment!
